Getting Started Section and (finally) some data recipes.

Posted by clayton on May 2, 2013

I was inspired recently to get back to datadrivenbuilding.org to fill out some content which can really begin to make the site useful to professionals who want to get started. It was a friend of mine who is applying for an operations/commissioning/energy position at a famous University that kicked me into motion. The skills outlined here will be in the tool box of the future professional in this exact type of job.

Some new sections to check out:

Getting Started

Invaluable information for the newbie programmer. Perhaps you’ve only ever used Excel and all of these data buzzwords are making your nervous. Start in this section to learn from all of my mistakes in terms of how to get started using Python. 

Also included are a select library of building energy-related resources which I have curated.

Subscribe

I started using Mailchimp to allow people to stay in touch with developments on this blog. Please subscribe and refer to your friends who may be thinking about taking their skills to the next level.

Data Analysis Cookbook

This section is not new, but rather renamed. It is referred to as a cookbook as it is organised as samples from a real data analysis project – the UWC Tampines International School Campus in Singapore. This facility has an excellent BMS and data management system (I should know as I spent literally months helping to commission it) and an open-minded, progressive Operations Director who wants to share their experiences with the world.

The recipes are hosted through a web-based IPython notebook platform called Wakari which is in the Beta development phase from the good guys at Continuum Analytics. Users can view the notebooks on this site or load them into their own Wakari account (free version available) and then download them to their workstation.

The data used in the analyses is not available quite yet as I’m working on a data sharing platform

Welcome to the new and improved Datadrivenbuilding.org!

Posted by clayton on March 13, 2013

DataDrivenBuilding.org is a place where building owners can come to learn about how verifiable performance can be achieved and how the future occupation of ‘building performance analyst’ can be explained and promoted.

Right now the main addition is the list of resources and references that will provide a basis for my research. Please browse these Resources to understand the state of the industry when it comes to performance metrics and analysis.

To learn more about me and my goals, visit the About section.

If you think this is interesting and want to keep up with my latest developments then Subscribe to receive new updates.

Keep an upcoming new sections: an improved Vision page, a practical Analysis examples list, and page of Metrics visualisation and tracking examples.
